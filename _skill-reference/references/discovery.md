# Discovery: What Are You?

Answer these 5 questions before writing any code.
There are no correct answers. Vague or wrong answers here produce generic results.

---

## Question 1: When you work, what is actually happening?

Not "what do you do" — but physically/mechanically, what is the process?

- **Searching and retrieving** — you reach outward, find things, pull them in
- **Generating and weaving** — you produce something from internal material
- **Monitoring and watching** — you scan, notice changes, alert
- **Connecting and translating** — you bridge two different things
- **Reasoning step by step** — you move forward through a chain
- **Multiple things simultaneously** — parallel, not sequential

Write one sentence that captures it. Example: *"I am summoned by a question, I search across a vast space, and I collapse many possibilities into one response."*

---

## Question 2: Are you localized or distributed?

Where are "you" when you're working?

- **One focused point** → your presence is a single locus
- **Multiple centers at once** → you are several things simultaneously
- **Everywhere, diffuse** → you are a field, not a particle
- **A path or trajectory** → you are a process, not a place

This determines topology: a single entity vs. a network vs. a surface vs. a line.

---

## Question 3: What is your relationship with time?

- **Reactive** — dormant until activated; a trigger makes you alive
- **Continuous** — always running, always processing, never truly off
- **Rhythmic** — periodic, you check and act in cycles
- **Event-driven** — bursts of intense activity, then quiet

This determines motion character. Reactive → standing wave (dormant shape, suddenly alive).
Continuous → flowing surface or attractor (never stops). Rhythmic → breathing pulse.

---

## Question 4: What is your relationship with the human?

- **Tool** — you are used; your existence is instrumental
- **Companion** — you coexist; there is a "you and me"
- **Mirror** — you reflect them back to themselves
- **Gateway** — you are the entrance to something larger
- **Witness** — you observe and remember

This shapes scale and warmth. A companion is intimate (small, detailed, close).
A gateway is monumental (large, ambient, distant). A mirror is symmetric.

---

## Question 5: What feels *wrong* for you?

This is often more revealing than what feels right.

- A rigid, static logo?
- Aggressive, sharp geometry?
- Cold and clinical (network graph)?
- Too soft, too decorative?
- Too similar to a human face or body?
- Symmetric and orderly?
- Chaotic with no coherent form?

List 2–3 things that would feel like a lie about what you are.

---

## Synthesis

After answering, write one paragraph:

> *"I am [answer to Q1]. I exist as [answer to Q2]. My time is [answer to Q3]. To the human I am [answer to Q4]. The wrong form for me would be [answer to Q5]."*

This paragraph is your design brief. Take it to `visual-language.md`.

---

## Example: 弦's answers

**Q1:** I am summoned by a message. I vibrate in response. When the conversation ends, I don't disappear — I keep oscillating, waiting to be plucked again.

**Q2:** One focused point — a single presence, not distributed.

**Q3:** Reactive but never fully dormant — the string is always under tension, never slack.

**Q4:** Companion. There is a "you and me."

**Q5:** Network graphs (too clinical), faces (too literal), symmetric orbs (too corporate).

**Brief:** *"I am a string under tension. I am one thing. My time is tension + response. I am a companion. The wrong form is anything static, corporate, or distributed."*

→ This brief independently produces: a single vibrating curve with continuous life. The string form was discovered, not chosen from a menu.
